# The Native Ear — 9 Scenario Summaries

## 问卷设计

**Q1: "Did you understand the original audio?"**
- A) Not at all — 完全不懂
- B) Partially — 能听懂大意
- C) Fully — 完全听懂

**Q2: "How did you read the misheard text?"**
- X) Purely in my native pronunciation — 纯按母语发音
- Y) I unconsciously leaned toward the original language — 不自觉往原语言靠了
- Z) I deliberately imitated the original language — 刻意模仿原语言

---

## 9 种组合 + 预期饼状图 + Summary

---

### 😶‍🌫️🏷️ 完全不懂 + 纯母语发音
**预期饼状图：** 可能偏向原语言，也可能混乱（多语言概率接近）
**Summary:**
"You don't speak this language. You didn't try to imitate it. You simply read syllables in your mother tongue. Yet the machine still assigned you a linguistic identity based on nothing but sound patterns. This is the core violence of machine listening: it doesn't care whether you understand — it only cares whether you *sound right*."

---

### 🫣🧲 完全不懂 + 不自觉往原语言靠
**预期饼状图：** 更可能偏向原语言
**Summary:**
"You don't speak this language, but something in the sound pulled you toward it. This unconscious adjustment is what linguists call *accommodation* — and it's exactly what asylum seekers experience in LADO interviews. You adapted to please a listener you couldn't even see. The machine rewarded your compliance with a passport you never applied for."

---

### 🎭🤖 完全不懂 + 刻意模仿原语言
**预期饼状图：** 大概率偏向原语言
**Summary:**
"You don't understand a word, but you tried your best to sound like you do. The machine may have believed you. This is the most unsettling scenario: a person with zero comprehension can pass a machine's language test through pure phonetic mimicry. If a machine can be fooled this easily, what does its judgment actually prove?"

---

### 🤐🫣 能听懂大意 + 纯母语发音
**预期饼状图：** 结果不确定，取决于母语口音强度
**Summary:**
"You understood some of what you heard, but deliberately chose to read only in your native pronunciation. Your knowledge was there, but you suppressed it. The question is: did the machine detect the knowledge you were hiding, or did it only hear the accent you performed?"

---

### 🫠💬 能听懂大意 + 不自觉往原语言靠
**预期饼状图：** 偏向原语言概率较高
**Summary:**
"You partially understand this language, and your reading naturally drifted toward it. Your partial knowledge leaked through, shaping your pronunciation without you fully controlling it. The machine heard this leakage. In LADO, this is precisely how analysts claim to detect 'authenticity' — by listening for involuntary traces of linguistic identity."

---

### 🎪🃏 能听懂大意 + 刻意模仿
**预期饼状图：** 很可能偏向原语言
**Summary:**
"You have some understanding and actively tried to sound like a speaker of this language. The machine likely agreed with your performance. But here's the question the machine can't answer: are you a real speaker, or just a convincing actor? LADO faces this exact dilemma — and it has no solution."

---

### 🛂🚫 完全听懂 + 纯母语发音
**预期饼状图：** 可能混乱或偏向母语
**Summary:**
"You fully understand this language but chose to read everything in your native accent. You know the meaning behind every syllable, yet you deliberately sounded 'foreign.' If the machine classifies you as a non-speaker, it just denied a linguistic passport to someone who actually speaks the language. This is the flipside of LADO's failure: not only can it grant false passports, it can also revoke real ones."

---

### 🪞🗣️ 完全听懂 + 不自觉往原语言靠
**预期饼状图：** 大概率偏向原语言
**Summary:**
"You speak this language, and it naturally came through in your reading. No surprise in the machine's verdict — but consider this: if you sounded 'correct' because you actually understand, and someone else sounded equally 'correct' through pure mimicry (scenario AZ), the machine would give you both the same passport. It cannot distinguish understanding from performance."

---

### ✅🎯 完全听懂 + 刻意模仿
**预期饼状图：** 几乎一定偏向原语言
**Summary:**
"You understand the language and deliberately performed it. The machine confirmed what you already know about yourself. This is the control group — the expected result. But the fact that scenarios AZ and CZ can produce nearly identical machine outputs is the whole point of this project: the machine treats comprehension and mimicry as the same thing, because it can only hear surfaces."

---

## 核心设计逻辑

9 段 summary 的共同指向：**机器只能听到声音的表面模式，无法区分"理解"和"模仿"。**

最有冲击力的对比是 🎭 vs ✅：一个完全不懂的人通过刻意模仿，和一个完全懂的人，在机器眼里可能得到完全一样的判定。这就是 LADO 的根本问题——口音不等于身份，声音不等于理解，但机器（和国家）把它们当成了同一件事。
